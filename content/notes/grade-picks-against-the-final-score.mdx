---
title: "Grade every prediction against the real final score"
date: "2026-07-05"
tags: ["evals", "honesty"]
---

A betting model is easy to make look good — publish the wins, quietly forget the losses. CrownWager does the opposite: **every published pick is settled against the real final score**, and the track record is flagged *insufficient* until at least 20 picks have settled.

That second rule matters as much as the first. A 7–2 record looks great and means almost nothing — small samples lie. Refusing to show a headline number until there's enough signal is the same discipline as everywhere else here: a result ships with the method and the sample that produced it, or it doesn't ship.

The honest version is less flattering and more trustworthy. See [CrownWager](/work/crownwager).
